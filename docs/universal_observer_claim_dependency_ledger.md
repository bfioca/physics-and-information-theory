## Decisive Quantum Bridge

The classical body inequalities closest to UO.2C bound angular momentum as a
classical charge. They do not automatically imply

```text
sum_i <J_i^2> <= function(E_prop,a,gravity margins).
```

That distinction is not cosmetic. A quantum state can have `<J_i>=0` for every
axis while retaining large Casimir, quantum Fisher information, and rotational
asymmetry. Therefore the combined theorem cannot be obtained by simply
substituting a classical `|J|` bound into a quantum-reference accuracy law.

The global operational part is closed without a QFI tail assumption:

```text
R_ref >= kappa_SO3(C2_ref)/2,
R_ref >= 1/(16 C2_ref+8),
R_ref >= 6/(e*pi^(5/3)) exp[-2 B(K1_ref)/3],
```

where the first line is Hayashi's known exact optimum and `B` is the Gibbs
entropy envelope with degeneracy `(2j+1)^2`.
Jensen also gives `K1_ref(K1_ref+1)<=<J^2>`. The confined-orbital theorem now
supplies the missing second-moment bridge for a named local matter class:

```text
H_ex>=sum_i p_i^2/(2m_i), |x_i|<=a
  => <L^2><=2Ma^2E_ex
  => R_ref>=1/(32Ma^2E_ex+8).
```

This does not stipulate `I=kappa m a^2`, particle number, polarization, or a
fixed irrep. It remains narrower than the target static-patch theorem because
it excludes intrinsic spin, relativistic fields, soft support, negative
interaction energy, and a derived gravitational compactness law.

The abstract spectral version makes the remaining decision precise. If the
sector floors `epsilon_j` have finite rotational partition function
`sum_j(2j+1)^2 exp(-beta epsilon_j)`, energy bounds global asymmetry. If they do
not grow, no such conclusion follows: the fixed positive invariant Hamiltonian
`H_bad=sum_j(1-e^-j)P_j<1` admits cutoff tokens with risk tending to zero at
uniformly bounded energy. The next physical task is therefore to derive the
sector floors from the supported matter action, not to add another kinematic
resource proxy. The hard-supported Skyrme hedgehog family now passes that test
at adiabatic collective level:

```text
I[F]<=[4/(3N_w)]M[F]a^2
  => E_j>=sqrt[3N_w j(j+1)/2]/a.
```

This is uniform over radial profile relaxation and makes the integer and
projective rotational partition functions finite. The remaining Skyrmion gap
is no longer the fixed-profile `Omega^4` coefficient for coercivity; it is a
comparison between the collective band and the complete rotating field/wall
spectrum, including projection error and any marked-wall inertia.

The exact comparison theorem is now also closed. If `a_j` is the collective
floor, the complement begins above `a_j+Delta_j`, and `||QHP_j||<=v_j`, then

```text
epsilon_j^full >= a_j-v_j^2/Delta_j.
```

```text

```

Second-order anticoherent states have zero mean angular momentum and isotropic

maximally mixed state on the same irrep has the same Casimir and no asymmetry.
Thus neither mean charge nor Casimir alone is an operational quality measure.
The tempting proof direction is

```text
small global Bayes risk -> directly required C2_ref -> localized energy cost.
```

The first arrow is only necessary, not sufficient. A reducible pure state with
spin-`j` probability `1/j` has fixed mean linear spin cost and
`Tr(F)=4(j+1)`, yet its whole orbit lies within trace distance `1/sqrt(j)` of an
invariant state. The preferred global route is therefore

```text
small global Bayes risk -> robust typical-spin/global-asymmetry resource

```

The QFI route requires an additional spectral or higher-moment tail condition.

  States](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0106014), optimize finite-spin frame
  alignment and obtain vanishing average error with increasing spin resources.
- Bartlett, Rudolph, Spekkens, and Turner, [Degradation of a Quantum Reference
  Frame](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0602069), define task-dependent
  longevity and find quadratic longevity scaling in their directional-frame
  model.
- Gour, Marvian, and Spekkens, [Measuring the Quality of a Quantum Reference
  Frame](https://arxiv.org/abs/0901.0943), establish relative entropy of
  frameness as an asymmetry quality measure.
- Gour and Spekkens, [The Resource Theory of Quantum Reference
  Frames](https://arxiv.org/abs/0711.0043), formulate orientation references as
  asymmetry resources, while Kudo and Tajima, [Fisher Information Matrix as a
  Resource Measure in Resource Theory of
  Asymmetry](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.03245), identify the QFI matrix as a
  connected-Lie-group asymmetry resource.
- Goldberg and James, [Quantum-Limited Euler Angle Measurements Using
  Anticoherent States](https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.02355), and Baguette and
  Martin, [Anticoherence Measures for Pure Spin
  States](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.01246), exhibit the zero-mean,
  high-sensitivity states that block a mean-angular-momentum substitution.
- Dain, [Inequality Between Size and Angular Momentum for
  Bodies](https://arxiv.org/abs/1305.6645), proves a size-angular-momentum
  inequality for an axisymmetric constant-density body class.
- Khuri and Xie, [Inequalities Between Size, Mass, Angular Momentum, and Charge
  for Axisymmetric Bodies](https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.04892), and Jaracz and
  Khuri, [Bekenstein Bounds, Penrose Inequalities, and Black Hole
  Formation](https://arxiv.org/abs/1802.04438), prove broader classical body
  inequalities under named geometric and energy hypotheses.
- Akhmedov et al., [Quantum Fields in the Static de Sitter
  Universe](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.13952), fix the distinguished
  de Sitter-invariant thermal static-patch two-point function.
- Chandrasekaran, Longo, Penington, and Witten, [An Algebra of Observables for
  de Sitter Space](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.10780), construct a gravitational
  observer algebra but do not supply the localization-reference-capacity
  inequality sought here.

The defensible novelty target is not any item above. It is a theorem that joins
an operational non-Abelian reference-quality requirement to a quantum
second-moment localization/gravity capacity and a static-patch coherence/locality
constraint, with one matter model realizing the common premises.
