```text
Produce a complete, sendable research-review packet for Dr. Daniel Harlow that
asks whether a finite localized directional record is a useful concrete model
of the observer subsystem appearing in quantum-gravity observer rules.

Do not stop at a plan or outline. Read the current repository, the colleague
status brief, the Harlow-facing paper goal, the universal-observer theorem
outline, and the relevant source-bound certificates. Then research and verify
the nearest literature using primary sources, write the packet, compile its
final PDF, perform a claim/evidence audit, and leave the repository with all
sendable artifacts complete.

The conceptual question is:

  Can one localized system in a de Sitter static patch acquire, retain, and
  expose a relational SO(3) orientation record whose directional information,
  record lifetime, support, energy cost, and gauge-invariant gravitational
  footprint are controlled in one semiclassical regime; and is this a useful
  concrete bridge to observer entropy and observer decoherence in quantum
  gravity?

Treat this as a request for conceptual critique and redirection, not an appeal
for endorsement, priority, collaboration, or publication approval.

Required framing:

1. Make the finite observer register the headline. Weight the main packet
   approximately 70% observer-register architecture, 25% gravitational witness,
   and 5% Skyrmion/profile certification.
2. Define the task as global-risk relational alignment between two localized
   physical systems. Never describe it as absolute orientation relative to a
   coordinate frame.
3. Define S_dir initially as operational directional-record information,
   represented by relative entropy of SO(3) asymmetry. Do not identify S_dir,
   effective record dimension, Casimir, QFI, thermodynamic entropy, or Harlow's
   S_Ob. State the proved comparison lemmas and make the missing S_dir-to-S_Ob
   dictionary the first review question.
4. Replace generic "long coherence" language with operational stability of a
   classical relational record after storage time T.
5. Present the fixed-background electric-Weyl response B_W only as evidence
   that the register is not gravitationally invisible. Do not assert that B_W
   is the final gravitational entropy bottleneck.
6. Present the supported Skyrmion only as a candidate local-matter witness.
   The observer-register architecture must survive rejection of the hard wall,
   membrane, or Skyrmion realization.
7. Mark the microscopic preparation/storage/readout channel as the primary
   conceptual bottleneck. Explain exactly what is proved, conditional, and
   open about the KMS-to-record-channel bridge.
8. Use one compact theorem sketch with explicit quantifiers over a declared
   model/state/protocol class. Clearly distinguish the proved abstract
   inequalities from the proposed one-action physical theorem.
9. Put detailed interval arithmetic, residual constants, and profile machinery
   in the optional technical appendix. They must support credibility without
   driving the opening narrative.
10. Make no novelty or priority claim that is not supported by a verified
    nearest-neighbor literature comparison.

Required artifacts:

A. A one-page conceptual note that can be read independently. It must contain:
   - the observer-level question;
   - why relational direction is a useful test register;
   - the schematic theorem chain;
   - what the current Skyrmion/Weyl work contributes;
   - the primary conceptual bottleneck; and
   - the precise reason Harlow's feedback is being requested.

   relational risk
       -> required directional information S_dir

       -> localization and gravity budgets
       -> record lifetime/readout error
       -> proposed observer tradeoff,

   with separate labels for proved, conditional, and open arrows. Show Paper R
   as a gravitational-witness branch and Paper A as realization support, not as
   the main chain.

C. A page containing exactly these three review questions, polished but not
   expanded into a larger list:

   1. Is SO(3) asymmetry/effective directional-record information meaningfully
      comparable to observer entropy in the observer rule, or are they
      fundamentally different resources?
   2. Which gauge-invariant gravitational quantity should cap the capacity of a
      finite observer register in a de Sitter causal patch: collapse, horizon or
      QES displacement, exterior response, or something else?
   3. Would a one-action semiclassical model deriving relational records,
      lifetime, localization, and gravitational footprint clarify the observer
      rule, or is the closed-universe gravitational path integral essential from
      the outset?

D. An optional technical appendix that records only the minimum evidence needed
   to establish seriousness:
   - the global SO(3) risk/asymmetry and risk/Casimir inequalities;
   - the conditional record-degradation theorem;
   - the distinction between proved orbital capacity and the Skyrmion

   - the certified profile/existence status;
   - the conserved-source and fixed-background response architecture;
   - the current zero-exclusion status and dominant remaining interval loss;
   - exact claim boundaries and reproducibility pointers.

E. A nearest-neighbor literature matrix using primary sources. Include at least:
   - the Harlow observer-rule papers motivating the question;
   - finite quantum reference frames and reference degradation;
   - relational/gravitating clocks or observers;
   - static-patch/de Sitter open-system dynamics;
   - gauge-invariant gravitational response;
   - curved-space or gravitating Skyrmions and rigorous-numerics precedents.
   For every proposed novelty claim, list the two or three closest results and
   state the narrower distinction without using "first" unless established.

F. A concise outreach email, at most 200 words, that:
   - makes clear why the question may intersect Harlow's work;
   - asks for conceptual direction rather than endorsement;
   - says what is attached and what can be ignored;
   - includes the three-question purpose in compressed form; and
   - does not burden the recipient with numerical details.

G. One compiled, visually clean PDF ordered as:
   1. conceptual note;
   2. dependency diagram;
   3. three questions;
   4. optional technical appendix;
   5. short bibliography.
   Also retain editable source files and the standalone outreach email.

Claim-discipline requirements:

- Use [PROVED], [CONDITIONAL], and [OPEN] consistently in the dependency
  diagram and technical appendix.
- State that the current fixed-background Weyl interval does not yet exclude
  zero unless that certificate is completed during this goal.
- State that the common-action physical record channel is open unless it is

- State that S_dir is not yet identified with S_Ob.
- State that self-consistent rotating Einstein-Skyrme geometry, full Israel

2. The main conceptual note contains no more than three equations and no
   implementation-level numerical constants.
3. A reader can identify the exact theorem domain, operational task, record
   variable, gravitational witness, and primary open bridge in under five
   minutes.
4. The observer-register architecture remains meaningful if every paragraph
   about the Skyrmion is removed.
5. Every technical claim maps to a theorem, source-bound artifact, or explicit
   OPEN label.
6. Every literature entry links to a primary source and has been checked rather
   than copied from prior generated feedback.
7. The PDF builds reproducibly, has no broken references or clipped diagram,
   and the editable sources match it.
8. An adversarial review explicitly checks for conflation of absolute and
   relational orientation, quantum coherence and classical record stability,
   S_dir and S_Ob, measurement back-action and gravitational backreaction, and
   fixed-background response and self-consistent gravity.
9. The outreach email and packet never ask Harlow to approve publication.
10. The final response reports the artifact paths, build/test commands, sources
    verified, unresolved factual uncertainties, and the exact claims still
    open. Do not mark the goal complete until the packet is genuinely ready to
    attach to an email.

Do not send the email or contact anyone. The terminal condition is a polished,
audited packet and email draft that the user can review and send themselves.
```

## Why this is the next goal

This milestone can be completed before the full one-action theorem. It asks
Harlow to evaluate the architecture at the point where redirection is still
cheap. It also forces the repository to produce a clean external narrative,
verify the literature independently, and expose the physical channel as the
main conceptual uncertainty.

Historically, the later completion target was
`harlow_facing_paper_goal.md`; the still later
`paper_u_information_exposure_goal.md` sprint ended in the generic novelty stop
recorded by `information_exposure_control_result.md`. This packet goal no
longer selects the research direction.
