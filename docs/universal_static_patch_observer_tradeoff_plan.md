# Universal Static-Patch Observer Tradeoff Research Plan

Status: primary theorem target; constituent model theorems partly complete

## Paper Thesis

The main paper should test the following class-uniform claim:

> A finite localized rotational reference in a static patch cannot
> simultaneously achieve arbitrarily accurate orientation recovery, long
> coherence, small optical support, and negligible gravitational
> backreaction.

The certified massive Skyrmion is not the class-uniform theorem. It is the
candidate matter realization that can make the assumptions nonempty once its
worldtube stress, lifetime, and metric response are derived from the same
action. This hierarchy gives two publishable outcomes:

1. a nonempty certified window gives a constructive observer model; or
2. an excluded open parameter region or controlled scaling family gives a
   quantitative localization-reference-backreaction obstruction under
   explicit hypotheses.

Recent work already treats finite-resource QRF backreaction, microscopic-clock
accuracy versus gravitational disturbance, physical scalar reference fields in
linearized gravity, and spectral admissibility of gapped de Sitter observers.
The novelty target is therefore specifically rotational: a global `SO(3)`
orientation-risk inequality that also prices optical localization and KMS
coherence with the same matter and gravitational ledger.

Failure at one parameter point is neither a universal no-go nor evidence for
one. Every negative result must exclude a declared family and separate a
physical obstruction from interval overestimation.

## Exact Claim Boundary

The repository does not yet prove the quoted universal statement. It currently
proves a controlled spherical-top/thin-shell branch obstruction and several
independent ingredients. A defensible theorem must declare which of the
following assumptions it uses:

- a primary reference-quality measure, with any Casimir, quantum Fisher
  information, or frameness proxy connected to it by a proved comparison;
- a local support measure, distinguishing proper enclosing radius, optical
  radius, and proper distance to the horizon;
- a coherence definition and a finite protocol time;
- a quasilocal/Killing-energy and angular-momentum or Casimir budget;
- an energy condition or constitutive bound controlling rotational inertia;
- a weak-backreaction criterion with a named geometric meaning; and
- a declared local interaction connecting the reference to the target.

No result may be called class-uniform over observers if it assumes the marked
spherical-top action, the append-and-twirl channel, or disjoint thin shells
without naming that restriction.

The first viable class is: localized stationary or adiabatic matter references
with a unitary `SO(3)` action, finite proper energy, a declared stress-energy
condition, a specified local KMS coupling, and a quantitative weak-field
criterion. "All observers" is not a meaningful theorem domain.

## Class-Uniform Inequality Program

### UO.1 Reference Requirement

Use covariant orientation Bayes risk as the primary quantity. For the
Haar-prior chordal task, the repository now proves

```text
R_ref >= 6/(e*pi^(5/3)) exp[-2 A_SO3(eta)/3]
      >= 6/(e*pi^(5/3)) exp[-2 B(K1_ref)/3],

R_ref >= 1/(16 C2_ref+8).
```

The last inequality is an independent spin-1 fusion/discrete-Hardy theorem.
Here `B(K)` is the exact Gibbs envelope generated by
`sum_j(2j+1)^2 exp(-beta j)`. The theorem covers arbitrary states, arbitrary
rotation-trivial multiplicities, arbitrary POVMs, and rare high-spin tails.
The remaining operational task is a separate implication between this
estimation risk and full-sector recovery for the physical target protocol. For
the declared spin-1 estimate-and-correct channel this comparison is now exact:

```text
(8/9)R_ref<=epsilon_rec<=min{1,2sqrt(R_ref)}.
```

Arbitrary coherent decoders and higher-spin targets remain separate tasks.
Existing
exact results already provide finite-multiplicity lower bounds, an all-state
mean-Casimir obstruction for the spherical rotor completion, a constructive
Peter-Weyl upper bound, and the failure of every single-irrep token for full
quantum recovery.

The global covariant risk-to-asymmetry/mean-spin target is complete. The missing
bridge is now physical: compare the estimation task with coherent recovery and
derive the mean-spin or second-moment energy capacity from a named local matter
class beyond the spherical-top ansatz. These quantities must not be treated as
interchangeable by notation.

The robust implication is direct and one-way:

```text
R_ref<=epsilon -> C2_ref>=(epsilon^-1-8)/16.
```

The converse fails. A maximally mixed state in a high-spin irrep has large
Casimir but no orientation asymmetry.

Local QFI is not sufficient either. A state with high-spin probability `1/j`
can have fixed mean linear spin cost and QFI growing as `j`, while its complete
rotation orbit converges in trace distance to an invariant state. Any QFI route
therefore needs a spectral or higher-moment tail condition.

### UO.2 Localization-Energy Capacity

For confined spinless nonrelativistic orbital matter, the first named-class
version is now proved. If every particle lies inside proper radius `a` and the
ground-subtracted Hamiltonian satisfies `H_ex>=sum_i p_i^2/(2m_i)`, then

```text
<L^2><=2 M a^2 E_ex,
R_ref>=1/(32 M a^2 E_ex+8).
```

The proof is all-state, controls rare high-spin tails, and is independent of
particle number and arbitrary rotation-trivial internal multiplicity. Under
the separately declared proxy `2G(M+E_ex)/a<=chi`, it gives
`<L^2><=chi^2 a^4/(8G^2)` without a rigid-top inertia law.

The class-uniform static-patch task is now to derive, from a named relativistic
energy condition or quasilocal inequality, an upper bound

```text
K1_ref <= K1_max(E_prop,a,beta_comp,beta_metric,beta_stress)
```

or, under a stronger tail condition, a `C2_ref`/QFI bound or a direct upper
bound on global rotational orbit distinguishability. The existing spherical-
top result and the confined-orbital theorem both give `C2_ref=O(a^4/G^2)` under
different declared premises. The supported Skyrmion now supplies a relativistic
collective-family alternative: `I<=[4/(3N_w)]Ma^2` implies a profile-relaxed
sector floor growing linearly in spin. The research task is to determine

This is the decisive extension beyond the proved orbital class. Classical size-angular-momentum inequalities

A state with `<J>=0` can still have large Casimir and strong orientation

1. extend the proved orbital representation-energy inequality controlling
   `K1_ref` or a smoothed typical-spin resource to relativistic/intrinsic-spin

4. narrow the theorem to semiclassical polarized references and state that
   zero-mean quantum tokens are outside its scope.

covariance and positivity alone cannot replace a physical spectral theorem.

### UO.5 Combined Theorem

The class-uniform composition is now exact once a declared observer class
supplies a capacity `C2_ref<=C_max(a,beta)`, a localization map
`a<=A(s_opt,rho,beta)`, and isotropic rotational heat exposure
`Gamma(T)=integral_0^T gamma(tau)d tau`:

```text
R_ref(T)>=3/4(1-exp(-2Gamma))
          +exp(-2Gamma)/[16 C_max(A,beta)+8].
```

For target risk `epsilon`, define

```text
delta_epsilon(Gamma)=3/4-(3/4-epsilon)exp(2Gamma).
```

Necessary conditions are `delta_epsilon>0` and
`C_max(A,beta)>=[delta_epsilon^-1-8]_+/16`. In particular,

```text
Gamma < (1/2) log[(3/4)/(3/4-epsilon)].
```

This is a noise-exposure bound, not a universal elapsed-time limit. A physical
coherence theorem must derive a nonzero exposure from the same readout action.

Eliminate the auxiliary budgets to obtain either

```text
R_ref >= Phi(L,s_opt/R,tau_coh/R,beta_comp,beta_metric,
             beta_stress,rho/R),
```

or a necessary lower bound on a named gravitational margin or `s_opt` at fixed error and
coherence. The strongest useful version is uniform over a declared matter and
channel class. A narrower theorem remains publishable if every premise has a
physical example and a sharp failure mode.

Every asymptotic statement must declare its path, for example target spin
`L -> infinity` at fixed `R^2/G`, fixed local coupling class, and fixed risk,
coherence, and weak-backreaction budgets.

The repository now has a first conditional elimination theorem for the


A closed interval is a rigorous no-go for that declared design. An open

## Ranked Fallback Theorems

### Optical Common-Mode No-Go

Promote the exact `y/sinh(y)` correlation defect, radial-smearing invariance,
and gradient tensor obstruction into a theorem for a declared class of local
stationary static-patch channels. This is analytically cleaner but presently
does not cover every rotational matter coupling.

### Boundary-Regularity Spectral Universality

Classify the form-factor exponent from endpoint order. The existing hard wall
has a quadratic weight zero and `p^-5`; generic finite pinning loses that
cancellation. Prove the general endpoint theorem and test smooth confinement
for superalgebraic decay.

### Conditional Normalized Skyrmion ULE Certificate

The authenticated AU.3b baseline is complete and supplies directed
fixed-background mass and inertia intervals. Its `P=64` Sobolev constants are
weaker than AU.3a because the conservative AU.2 tail dominates. Refine that
tail and the bare absolute-value product bounds before using the constants in a
construction test. Poor constants become scientifically useful only after
timing and lifetime lower bounds are imposed and the failure is traced to invariant
combinations of localization, inertia, redshift, energy, and boundary
regularity rather than interval overestimation.

## Falsification Rules

Abandon or narrow the class-uniform claim if:

- zero-mean high-Casimir references evade every physically justified
  second-moment or representation-energy inequality;
- a credible local channel achieves the required common mode at fixed optical
  separation;
- coherence can be maintained independently of the energy/inertia variables;
- the Skyrmion window closes only because of avoidable interval overestimation;
  or
- the combined theorem is subsumed by an existing reference-frame or
  gravitational size inequality after translating notation.
