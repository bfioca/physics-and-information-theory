## Selected Paper

Working title:

> Computer-Assisted Existence and Fixed-Wall Radial Stability of a
> Dirichlet-Confined Massive Skyrmion

Central theorem:

> For `mu^2=1`, `lambda=1/400`, and Dirichlet radius `x_w=4`, the prescribed
> fixed-background hedgehog boundary-value problem has a unique solution in the
> certified augmented Newton ball of radius `1/250`. The solution is strictly
> decreasing, has strictly negative wall slope and finite positive rotor
> inertia, and obeys the certified radial stability floor. Its exact
> inertia-density transform has a nonzero oscillatory `p^-3` tail
> determined by the wall second jet. The invertible certified linearization
> also yields a locally unique real-analytic solution branch on some open
> neighborhood of the three model parameters.

The word "unique" is always local to the stated Newton neighborhood. The paper
does not claim global uniqueness.

## Why This Package

The profile theorem alone is mathematically complete but physically narrow.
The radial gap and spectral tail make the certified solution do useful work:

1. monotonicity, wall slope, and inertia show that the solution is a genuine
   rotational collective-coordinate candidate rather than only a numerical
   BVP root;
2. the authenticated radial gap gives a first dynamical stability statement on
   the declared fixed-wall domain; and
3. the sharp intrinsic `p^-3` coefficient exposes exactly how the imposed boundary enters
   the finite optical inertia transform.

The third point answers, rather than hides, the main modeling criticism. The
paper says that the Dirichlet wall is an imposed mathematical boundary
representing external support; it does not derive support traction or wall
dynamics. Standard endpoint asymptotics explain the power-law mechanism. The
contribution is the certified coefficient and global derivative envelope for
the exact nonlinear solution, not a bath or ultraviolet prediction for an
unsupported Skyrmion.

## Claim Boundary

- a self-gravitating Einstein-Skyrme-de Sitter solution;
- a dynamical or nonspherical stability theorem;
- a physical membrane completion or junction-condition solution;
- global uniqueness of the hard-wall profile;
- robustness under arbitrary smooth confinement;
- a bath factor, KMS reduced channel, observer algebra, or gravitational backreaction
  theorem; or

Those boundaries are part of the result's credibility, not footnotes to be
softened during manuscript drafting.
