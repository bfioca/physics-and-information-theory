# Post-Germ Indicial Inverse Majorant

After the exact Frobenius germs have been fixed through physical power `x^5`,
the remaining odd powers begin at `p=7`. A Taylor-majorant proof needs a
uniform inverse bound for every subsequent indicial matrix, not merely a proof
that its determinant is nonzero.

For the authenticated AU.3b slope interval, the exact checker writes

```text
K(p)=K2 p^2+K1 p+K0
```

and interval-encloses the absolute row sums of the adjugate coefficient
matrices. The already proved determinant identity gives

```text
det K(p)=det(P0)(p-1)(p-3)(p+2)(p+4).
```

The two lowest remaining powers are treated directly. For every odd `p>=11`,
the proof uses

```text
(p-1)(p-3)(p+2)(p+4) >= (80/121)p^4
```

and

```text
||adj K(p)||_infinity
 <= (N2+N1/11+N0/121)p^2.
```

This yields the exact rational bounds

```text
p=7:      ||K(p)^-1||_infinity < 0.078324234
p=9:      ||K(p)^-1||_infinity < 0.042058296
odd p>=11:||K(p)^-1||_infinity < 0.046377156
```

and therefore

```text
sup_{b in B_AU3b, odd p>=7} ||K_b(p)^-1||_infinity
  < 0.078324234 < 79/1000.
```

The maximum is the direct `p=7` bound. All arithmetic, including the slope
interval and principal-determinant lower bound, is exact rational interval
arithmetic.

## Role In The Proof

This constant is the linear Green majorant for the remaining Taylor
coefficients. It gives substantial room for a Neumann argument, but it does
not itself prove contraction. The next certificate must bound the normalized
nonleading operator and source convolutions generated by the authenticated
profile tube. Those bounds must retain the correlations responsible for the
origin cancellations.

## Claim Boundary

This theorem controls only the leading indicial inverse on post-germ powers.
It is not a bound on the full variable-coefficient operator, a Taylor
remainder, a finite-cell transfer, or a continuum inverse.
