# Harlow Redirection Branches

Status: historical conceptual-pivot map, not a set of active Git branches. The
generic information-exposure route subsequently ended in a novelty stop; see
`docs/information_exposure_control_result.md`.

These three pivots are
mutually distinguishable; none treats a critical response as a request to
rename the same claim.

## Branch 1. Operational directional-record theorem

**Trigger.** Harlow judges that relative entropy of `SO(3)` asymmetry and
`S_Ob` are fundamentally different resources, or that no useful comparison is
likely.

**Keep.** The exact relational task, `C_dir` protocol boundary, global
risk-to-information theorem, joint asymmetry bound, conditional finite
classical-record corollary, representation requirements, and named spectral or
orbital energy costs.

**Remove.** Any suggestion that `S_dir` measures observer Hilbert-space size,
closed-universe observer entropy, generalized entropy, or the accuracy exponent
in the observer rule.

**Replacement claim.**

> In a declared finite rotational-register protocol, target full-frame risk
> requires a minimum readout information and joint `SO(3)` asymmetry; a finite
> classical storage bottleneck and specified matter Hamiltonian turn those
> necessities into explicit record-size and energy costs.

**Next bounded result.** Sharpen the `SO(3)` rate-distortion dual bound or prove
a matching achievability theorem, state the classical bottleneck theorem with
postselection, and optimize the spectral energy inversion for one non-Skyrmion
matter class. Gravity may remain as motivation or a separately labeled
compactness proxy.

**Success criterion.** A theorem paper whose abstract contains no `S_Ob` and
whose novelty is judged against finite-QRF and physical-memory literature.

**Stop criterion.** If the rate-distortion/classical-record step is already a
directly published theorem at the same scope and the matter specialization adds
no new bound, do not market the combination as a new physics theorem.

## Branch 2. Gravitational-functional selection

**Trigger.** Harlow accepts the finite-register question but rejects the chosen
compactness, curvature, horizon, or response variable as the quantity that
should constrain observer capacity.

**Keep.** U1-U7 from the manuscript skeleton and the exact separation between
register energy, total Killing charge, proper support, optical support, and
gravitational disturbance.

**Freeze.** No candidate `G_cap` appears in the headline inequality. The
compactness proxy remains a named matter corollary, and Paper R remains an
`INCONCLUSIVE STOP` diagnostic.

**Replacement program.** Compare, on one common localized stress family, only
the candidates that Harlow identifies as meaningful. Each candidate must have:

1. a gauge-invariant or relational definition;
2. a reason it limits observer capacity rather than merely detects matter;
3. a stress-to-functional estimate on the same state and perturbative order;
4. a controlled zero or weak-backreaction limit; and
5. compatibility with the support and energy ledgers in `C_dir`.

Possible categories, not assumed equivalents, are collapse/trapped-surface
admissibility, horizon or QES displacement, an observer-algebra entropy or
relative-entropy budget, and a local tidal-disturbance norm.

**Next bounded result.** A short selection note with one theorem or decisive
counterexample per candidate, ending in one chosen functional or an explicit
statement that the operational theorem currently has no justified gravity
closure.

**Success criterion.** Exactly one functional has a common-action bound and a
clear operational role, allowing U9 to be restated without proxy language.

**Stop criterion.** If no candidate can be related to directional capacity
without self-consistent gravity or a closed-universe construction, publish or
circulate only the non-gravitational theorem and leave the gravity arrow open.

## Branch 3. Algebraic or closed-universe translation test

**Trigger.** Harlow judges that a localized semiclassical register in one
static patch does not clarify the observer rule, because the closed-universe
path integral, observer conditioning, or Type-II algebra is essential from the
outset.

**Keep.** Only the operational question and the proved group-orbit
rate-distortion theorem. Treat the finite register as a test probe, not as a
model of the complete observer.

**Retire from the main line.** The Skyrmion realization, membrane support,
fixed-background response, and any claim that a local register underwrites
`S_Ob`.

**Translation question.** Given a named observer algebra or observer-conditioned
corner with an `SO(3)` action and conditional expectation `E_SO3`, determine
whether

```text
D(omega || omega o E_SO3)
```

is finite, operationally monotone for allowed observer instruments, and an
upper bound on the information in a relational orientation readout. This is a
comparison problem inside the algebraic framework; it does not assume equality
with its trace entropy or `S_Ob`.

**Next bounded result.** Specify one algebra, one normal-state class, one
covariant instrument, and one loss. Prove either an algebraic data-processing
version of U1-U2 or a counterexample showing that the finite-register resource
does not survive observer conditioning.

**Success criterion.** A precise theorem or no-go that tells Harlow's framework
whether a compact rotational reference sector contributes an independently
measurable operational resource.

**Stop criterion.** If the required state, conditional expectation, or
instrument cannot be defined without solving the full path integral, record
that obstruction and keep the G1/G2 result as a standalone finite-QRF theorem.

## Response format after feedback

Whichever branch is triggered, revise the project in this order:

1. quote the conceptual objection in one sentence;
2. state which old implication is deleted or frozen;
3. state the replacement theorem domain;
4. list the one next bounded proof object; and
5. give a falsifiable stop criterion.

This keeps expert feedback capable of changing the research direction rather
than merely changing its vocabulary.
