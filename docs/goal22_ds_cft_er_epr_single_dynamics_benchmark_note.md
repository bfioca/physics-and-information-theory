# Goal 22: Finite dS/CFT-ER=EPR Single-Dynamics Benchmark

Goal 22 upgrades Goal 21 from a screen-shadow comparison to a single finite
dynamics family. The same finite screen operator-transfer process generates
both:

- the screen-visible data; and
- the observer bridge channel.

The result is still a no-go with a completion theorem inside the finite family:

```text
screen-restricted dynamics can hide bridge algebra;
full intrinsic operator dynamics recovers it.
```

## Transfer Family

For every `d >= 2`, use a finite operator-transfer process on `M_d` with one
parameter:

```text
offdiag_coupling = 1: off-diagonal matrix units are fixed
offdiag_coupling = 0: off-diagonal matrix units are projected to zero
```

Diagonal matrix units are fixed in both cases. Thus the screen/horizon algebra
`C^d` sees the same transfer process, while the induced bridge channel differs:

```text
offdiag_coupling = 1: identity on M_d
offdiag_coupling = 0: complete dephasing onto C^d
```

The bridge channel is induced by the same transfer map; it is not appended after
the screen shadow.

## No-Go

The two realizations agree on:

- screen entropies;
- diagonal correlator shadows;
- horizon-overlap data;
- low-order screen correlators;
- transfer spectra restricted to `C^d`.

But they induce different maximal bridge algebras:

```text
quantum bridge:          M_d
classical horizon bridge: C^d
```

So finite screen-restricted dynamics do not determine algebraic bridge
connectivity, even when generated by one declared transfer family.

## Intrinsic Completion

The completion diagnostic is full intrinsic operator dynamics:

- full operator transfer spectrum;
- off-diagonal relative-entropy response;
- commutator/OTOC-style growth from off-diagonal probes into the diagonal
  screen algebra.

These separate the two realizations and determine the bridge algebra inside
this finite family.

## Relation To Goals 19-21

Goal 19 works when Pauli probes are complete. Goal 20 shows diagonal probes can
miss maximal recoverable algebra. Goal 21 translates that into a finite
dS/CFT-screen shadow obstruction. Goal 22 strengthens the obstruction by making
the screen data and bridge channel outputs of one finite transfer dynamics.

## Claim Boundary

This is a finite QEC/OA-QEC dynamics benchmark. It is not a continuum dS/CFT
theorem, not de Sitter quantum gravity, not a type-III algebra theorem, and not
a proof of ER=EPR in de Sitter.
