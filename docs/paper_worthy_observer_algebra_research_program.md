## Completion Standard

The goal is complete only when all of the following exist:

1. A regulator whose Hilbert space, observable algebra, state, screen map, and
   dynamics are derived from one declared physical model.
2. Canonical refinement or coarse-graining maps with proved covariance and
   convergence on a specified operator domain.
3. A quantitative completeness or obstruction theorem using approximate,
   operational distances rather than exact equality of declared booleans.
4. An explicit recovery map or a rigorous impossibility bound in a stated norm.
5. A continuum or controlled large-cutoff result.
6. A novel physical consequence: a scaling law, invariant, reconstruction
   threshold, edge-mode correction, or discriminating no-go theorem.
7. Independent expert review and a paper draft whose central theorem survives
   the novelty and claim-boundary audit.

Passing the existing finite test suite is necessary but not sufficient.
