# Primary-Source Literature Matrix

Status: packet-ready literature audit
Checked through: 2026-06-10
Scope: nearest neighbors for the proposed finite directional-record program

Date convention: journal publication year where available, otherwise initial
arXiv year; a preprint/revision year is shown as well when it matters to the
current-state comparison.

## Bottom line

The literature already contains finite observer rules, observer-dependent
gravitational algebras and entropies, finite and degrading quantum reference
frames, operational clock/backreaction inequalities, de Sitter detector master
equations, gauge-invariant perturbation theory, curved and gravitating
Skyrmions, and computer-assisted boundary-value proofs. The packet therefore
makes no claim that any one of those ingredients is new.

The narrower research opening is to ask whether one declared local model can
put the following quantities on a common state family, protocol, parameter
window, and error ledger:

```text
global-risk relational SO(3) alignment
  + an operational rotational-asymmetry resource
  + operational record stability after a storage time
  + proper and optical localization
  + energy/stress cost
  + a gauge-invariant gravitational witness.
```

This audit establishes relevance, not priority. No use of "first" is
supported. In particular, `S_dir` is not identified with Harlow's `S_Ob`,
finite-reference measurement back-action is not gravitational backreaction,
and a fixed-background Weyl response is not self-consistent gravity.

## 1. Observer rules and gravitational observer algebras

| Primary source | Verified contribution | Relevance and narrow distinction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Daniel Harlow, Mykhaylo Usatyuk, and Ying Zhao, [*Quantum mechanics and observers for gravity in a closed universe*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.02359) (2025) | Proposes that the experiences of an observer `Ob` are approximately described by a Hilbert space of dimension roughly `exp(S_Ob)`, with errors exponentially small in `S_Ob`; gives gravitational-path-integral and coding evidence. | This is the main motivation for asking whether a concrete register can underwrite part of an observer's operational capacity. It does **not** equate `S_Ob` with relative entropy of `SO(3)` asymmetry or derive a localized directional-memory channel. |
| Daniel Harlow, [*Observers, alpha-parameters, and the Hartle-Hawking state*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03835) (2026) | Extends the observer-decoherence rule to theories where observers can fluctuate into existence in the Hartle-Hawking state and studies observer-conditioned cosmological questions. | Shows that the target framework is intrinsically closed-universe and path-integral based. The proposed static-patch register is only a possible semiclassical model beneath some observer assumptions, not a derivation of this rule. |
| Venkatesa Chandrasekaran, Roberto Longo, Geoff Penington, and Edward Witten, [*An Algebra of Observables for de Sitter Space*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.10780) (2022; JHEP 2023) | Constructs a static-patch observer-dressed Type `II_1` algebra and relates its entropy, up to a state-independent constant, to generalized entropy. | Establishes that explicit observer degrees of freedom and gravitational dressing matter in de Sitter. It does not provide a finite rotational register, an `SO(3)` estimation risk, or a microscopic record-lifetime model. |
| Edward Witten, [*Algebras, Regions, and Observers*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.02837) (2023) | Argues for observer-worldline algebras as an operational substitute for region algebras in gravity, with the de Sitter static patch as a central example. | Motivates the observer-centered framing. It is algebraic and does not derive a localized matter register's information, support, and stress budgets. |
| Julian De Vuyst, Stefan Eccles, Philipp A. Hoehn, and Josh Kirklin, [*Gravitational entropy is observer-dependent*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.00114) (2024; JHEP 2025) | Uses QRF methods to show that different observers can induce different physical algebras, density operators, and entropies; identifies the observers in the crossed-product construction with QRFs. | Makes it especially unsafe to identify `S_dir` with a unique gravitational observer entropy. The packet instead asks whether a comparison inequality exists for its declared observer and task. |
| Bin Chen and Jie Xu, [*An algebra for covariant observers in de Sitter space*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.00622) (2025; v2 2026) | Imposes all `SO(1,d)` constraints using covariant observers in superpositions of geodesics, gives an observer action model, and constructs an averaged Type-II algebra over fluctuating static patches. | This is a direct escape route from a fixed-patch compact-rotation model. The proposed project is narrower and operational: a finite relational `SO(3)` register in one patch. It must not claim a full de Sitter covariant-observer algebra. |

## 2. Finite quantum reference frames and degradation

| E. Bagan, M. Baig, and R. Muñoz-Tapia, [*Aligning Reference Frames Using Quantum States*](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0106014) (2001) | Solves an optimal finite-spin encoding and measurement problem for transmitting an orthogonal trihedron, with asymptotic average-error scaling. | Global spatial-frame estimation with finite spin resources is prior art. The packet's `T`-dependent classical-record stability must be distinguished from this repeated-use back-action and from gravitational backreaction. |
| Gilad Gour and Robert W. | Gilad Gour, Iman Marvian, and Robert W. Spekkens, [*Measuring the quality of a quantum reference frame: the relative entropy of frameness*](https://arxiv.org/abs/0901.0943) (2009) | Defines relative entropy of frameness, proves that it equals `G`-asymmetry, and bounds the group-element information extractable from a token. | This is the direct prior source for using relative entropy of `SO(3)` asymmetry as `S_dir`. | Stephen D. Bartlett, Terry Rudolph, Robert W. Spekkens, and Peter S. Turner, [*Quantum communication using a bounded-size quantum reference frame*](https://arxiv.org/abs/0812.5040) (2009) | Characterizes effective noise and decoding with bounded phase, Cartesian, and directional tokens, including measure-and-reorient and relational-subsystem implementations. | Bounded reference tokens and their recovery channels are established. The proposed work must add a matter-derived Hamiltonian, local KMS channel, localization, and gravitational witness on the same model. |
| Sébastien Christophe Garmier, Ladina Hausmann, and Esteban Castro-Ruiz, [*The Perspectives of Non-Ideal Quantum Reference Frames*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.19343) (2025) | Constructs perspectives for finite-resource non-ideal QRFs; finds representation-dependent superselection and use-induced operational back-action. | Finite QRF resources and QRF back-action are not new claims. The remaining distinction is a quantitative rotational risk-to-gravitational-disturbance chain; here "back-action" is operational, not automatically metric backreaction. |
| Anne-Catherine de la Hamette, [*Observer-Dependent Entropy and Diagonal Rényi Invariants in Quantum Reference Frames*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23598) (2026) | Bounds non-ideal-frame entropy differences using the representation-determined dimension of an effective relational Hilbert space. | This is a close neighbor to any proposed `effective record dimension -> observer-dependent entropy` statement. It does not supply Harlow's closed-universe `S_Ob` dictionary or a gravitational capacity bound. |

## Safe wording for the packet

> Existing work treats finite observer entropy, observer-dependent gravitational
> algebras, finite and degrading reference frames, physical clock limits, and
> de Sitter open-system dynamics. We ask whether these ingredients can be made
> quantitatively compatible in one deliberately narrow sector: a localized
> relational directional record with a controlled gravitational budget. We do
> not yet know whether its directional asymmetry is related to observer entropy
> or which invariant should replace or refine the provisional fixed-background
> Weyl witness.

  central claims were also checked in author-source text or arXiv HTML.
- A targeted arXiv query returned no paper matching both `Skyrmion` and
  `computer-assisted`, but this is weak negative evidence and is **not** a
  priority result.
- Journal databases, citation graphs, books, and differently worded non-arXiv
  work were not exhaustively searched.
- Specialist review is therefore required before any novelty sentence is used
  in a manuscript or outreach email.
