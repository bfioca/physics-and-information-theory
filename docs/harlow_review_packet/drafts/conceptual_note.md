# Finite Directional Records as a Concrete Observer Model

## The question

Can a finite localized system in a de Sitter static patch function as an
observer register in a fully operational sense: acquire a relational
orientation record, retain it for a stated proper time, and expose it to a
second localized system, while its information, support, energy cost, and
gravitational footprint are controlled in one semiclassical regime?

Quantum-gravity observer rules assign finite operational content to an
observer subsystem, while de Sitter observer algebras include the observer in
the construction. We ask whether a narrow physical register can make some of
those assumptions auditable. The aim is not to identify a rotor with an
observer, but to expose which information, dynamics, localization, and
gravitational inputs an observer rule needs.

## Why a directional record

We use a wholly relational task. Let `O` be the register and `D` a target
carrying physical orientation markers. The unknown datum is the group element
aligning them, not either system's orientation relative to coordinates:

```text
g_rel = g_O^{-1} g_D in SO(3).                                      (1)
```

The performance criterion is the Haar-prior global risk of estimating
`g_rel`. It probes the full rotational orbit, unlike local sensitivity or
Hilbert-space size alone.

For a covariant orientation ensemble, our starting information resource is
the relative entropy of rotational asymmetry,

```text
S_dir := D(rho_O || G_SO(3)[rho_O])
       = S(G_SO(3)[rho_O]) - S(rho_O).                              (2)
```

Here `G_SO(3)` is rotational twirling. `S_dir` is relative entropy of
rotational asymmetry. It is not identified with classical memory dimension,
thermodynamic entropy, Hilbert-space dimension, Casimir, quantum Fisher
information, or the observer entropy `S_Ob`. Whether comparison with `S_Ob`
is meaningful is the first review question.

Record lifetime is operational: after time `T`, a permitted relational
readout must still attain the declared risk. This is classical-record
stability, not generic phase coherence.

## The proposed theorem architecture

Fix a class `C_dir` of localized register-target models, states, environments,
actions, and preparation, storage, and readout protocols. The intended theorem
quantifies over this class. Schematically,

```text
R_rel(T) >= F_record(S_dir, degradation, channel error),
S_dir    <= F_capacity(energy, support, gravity; model data)
      => Phi(R_rel(T), T, support, gravity; model data) >= 0.        (3)
```

Rigorous arrows exist on named domains. Global relational risk requires
rotational resources; confined orbital matter converts them into support and
excitation-energy cost; and a stipulated isotropic heat channel gives a
conditional degradation law. These are global statements, not
local-sensitivity proxies.

The central open bridge is physical. One local action must derive acquisition,
storage degradation, and readout; bound the microscopic KMS-to-effective
channel error; and enter the same stress-energy ledger. The degradation
theorem is conditional on that channel, so we do not yet claim a physical
observer theorem.

## What gravity contributes

The gravitational branch asks whether the same register is visible to a
gauge-invariant exterior observable. On fixed de Sitter, exact
conserved-source, transmission, and electric-Weyl maps define a normalized
quadrupole witness `B_W`. This is fixed-background response, not a
self-consistent gravitating solution or completed junction analysis.

`B_W` is not the proposed capacity bound. No rigorous completed-source
interval presently excludes zero. Even after zero exclusion, collapse,
horizon or quantum-extremal-surface displacement, or another invariant may be
more relevant. We seek guidance on which quantity belongs in the
observer-level statement.

A supported massive Skyrmion supplies one certified matter candidate. It shows
only that the supported-profile subproblem is nonempty; the architecture
should survive rejection of this wall, membrane, or field model.

## Why request feedback now

The project is at a fork before its most expensive bridge. We seek direction
on the `S_dir`-to-`S_Ob` relation, the gravitational quantity constraining a
finite register, and whether a one-action semiclassical model clarifies the
observer rule without starting from a closed-universe path integral. We seek
criticism and redirection, not endorsement or a publication decision.
