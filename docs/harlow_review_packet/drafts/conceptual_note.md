# Finite Directional Records as a Concrete Observer Model

## The question

Can a finite localized system in a de Sitter static patch function as an
observer register in a fully operational sense: acquire a relational
orientation record, retain it for a stated proper time, and expose it to a
second localized system, while its information, support, energy cost, and
gravitational footprint are controlled in one semiclassical regime?

We are developing this as a concrete test of the observer subsystem that
appears abstractly in quantum-gravity observer rules. The aim is not to
identify a rotor with an observer. It is to ask whether a sharply defined
candidate record resource can expose which information, dynamics,
localization, and gravitational inputs an observer rule actually needs.

## Why a directional record

A direction is a useful minimal register because the task can be made wholly
relational. Let `O` be the register and `D` a target carrying physical
orientation markers. The unknown datum is the group element aligning them,
not either system's orientation relative to coordinates:

```text
g_rel = g_O^{-1} g_D in SO(3).                                      (1)
```

The performance criterion is the Haar-prior global risk of estimating
`g_rel`. It probes the full rotational orbit and separates a useful record
from a large but rotationally invariant Hilbert space.

For a covariant orientation ensemble, our starting information resource is
the relative entropy of rotational asymmetry,

```text
S_dir := D(rho_O || G_SO(3)[rho_O])
       = S(G_SO(3)[rho_O]) - S(rho_O).                              (2)
```

Here `G_SO(3)` is rotational twirling. For this declared task, `S_dir` is an
operational rotational-asymmetry resource. It is not identified here with an
effective classical record dimension, thermodynamic entropy, Hilbert-space
dimension, Casimir, quantum Fisher information, or the observer entropy
`S_Ob`. Whether any comparison with `S_Ob` is meaningful is one reason for
requesting feedback.

Record lifetime is also operational: after storage time `T`, an allowed
relational readout must still attain a declared risk. This is stability of a
classical record, not generic quantum phase coherence.

## The proposed theorem architecture

Fix a class `C_dir` containing a localized register and target, a static-patch
field environment in a specified KMS state, a local action, a controlled state
family, and preparation, storage, and readout protocols. The intended theorem
quantifies over every declared model, state, and allowed protocol in this
class. Schematically,

```text
R_rel(T) >= F_record(S_dir, degradation, channel error),
S_dir    <= F_capacity(energy, support, gravity; model data)
      => Phi(R_rel(T), T, support, gravity; model data) >= 0.        (3)
```

Several abstract arrows are rigorous. Global relational risk requires
rotational asymmetry and angular-momentum resources; a confined orbital-matter
class converts angular resource into localization and excitation-energy cost;
and a stipulated isotropic heat channel gives a conditional finite-time risk
bound. These are global statements, not local-sensitivity proxies.

The central open bridge is physical. One local action must justify preparation,
storage degradation, and readout; control the error between microscopic KMS
dynamics and the effective record channel; and enter the same stress-energy
ledger. The degradation theorem is currently conditional on that channel, so
we do not yet claim a physical observer theorem. The observer rule may require
this microscopic bridge, a weaker operational one, or a different record
variable.

## What gravity contributes

The gravitational branch asks whether the same register is visible to a
gauge-invariant exterior observable. On fixed de Sitter we use a normalized
electric-Weyl quadrupole response, `B_W`, derived from conserved
bulk-plus-support stress. It is a candidate witness that the register is not
gravitationally invisible. This is fixed-background response, not a
self-consistent gravitating solution or completed junction analysis.

`B_W` is not the proposed capacity bound. A source-bound floating target is
stable and nonzero, but no rigorous continuum interval for the completed
source presently excludes zero. Even after zero exclusion, collapse, horizon
or quantum-extremal-surface displacement, or another invariant may be more
relevant. We seek guidance on which quantity belongs in the observer-level
statement.

A supported massive Skyrmion supplies one certified local-matter candidate. It
shows that the supported-profile subproblem is nonempty, not that the observer
realization is complete; the architecture should survive rejection of this
hard-wall, membrane, or field model.

## Why request feedback now

The project is at a fork before the most expensive bridge is built. We would
value criticism of three choices: whether directional asymmetry is comparable
to observer entropy, which gravitational invariant should constrain a finite
register, and whether a one-action semiclassical record model clarifies the
observer rule or instead requires a closed-universe path integral. The
technical material shows the controlled basis for these questions; it is not
a request for endorsement of the current realization.
