# Finite dS-Like QEC Capstone Memo

## Certified Claim

We construct an exact finite stabilizer/QEC toy model satisfying a
de Sitter-like observer-patch diagnostic gate. The model has two finite
observer causal patches sharing a finite horizon. The two code realizations
agree on named patch entropy, overlap, MI/CMI/I3, and shared-horizon algebra,
but differ in observer-patch logical reconstruction. Exact erasure and channel
diagnostics certify that the distinction is operator-algebraic and
channel-semantic, not visible to horizon entropy alone.

The claim is deliberately finite and diagnostic. It is not a claim to construct
continuum de Sitter space.

## dS-Like Diagnostic Gate

The capstone gate requires:

- no privileged asymptotic boundary;
- primitive regions are observer causal patches, not boundary intervals;
- observer patches overlap on a finite shared horizon;
- the observer union forms a finite static diamond;
- horizon entropy and shared-horizon algebra are exact stabilizer diagnostics;
- observer reconstruction algebra is checked exactly;
- erasure semantics satisfy the QEC complementarity identity;
- channel/fixed-point behavior is certified by exact finite transition rules.

The certified model passes this gate through the `desitter-toy` capstone
certificate.

## Balanced-Bridge Observer-Patch Construction

The seed is the balanced-bridge CSS family `A_m,B_m`. For bridge pair
`p_j,q_j`, both codes receive the same checks:

```text
Z_1 Z_2 Z_{p_j} Z_{q_j}
X_0 X_5 X_{p_j} X_{q_j}
```

The observer-patch cover is:

```text
observer_p      = {1,2,3} union {p_j}
observer_q      = {1,2,3} union {q_j}
shared_horizon  = {1,2,3}
bridge_shell    = {p_j,q_j}
static_diamond  = observer_p union observer_q
```

The two observers overlap exactly on `shared_horizon`. This is the no-cloning
guard: when the second code gives both observers full logical reconstruction,
they are not disjoint copies.

## Main Witness

For every checked bridge slice, the two realizations agree on:

- labeled low-order entropy data;
- named patch entropy and overlap data;
- observer-pair MI/CMI/I3;
- shared-horizon algebra.

They differ on observer reconstruction:

```text
A_m observer algebra: (1,1,1,false)
B_m observer algebra: (2,0,0,true)
shared horizon:       (1,1,1,false)
```

The erasure suite verifies that private observer-shell erasures are correctable,
shared-horizon erasure is not correctable in the balanced-bridge model, and an
erased region is correctable exactly when the survivor complement reconstructs
all logical information.

## Relation To Goals 1-3

Goal 1 proves the algebraic seed: the balanced-bridge CSS family has matching
labeled one- and two-qubit entropy diagnostics, but different logical
reconstruction/algebra profiles.

Goal 2 turns that seed into finite causal-patch / horizon-code toy cosmologies:
named patch entropy, overlap, MI/CMI/I3, and shared-horizon-visible data can
match while observer-patch reconstructability, erasure semantics, and channel
behavior differ.

Goal 3 shows the same warning survives in holographic-looking
tensor-network/min-cut constructions: boundary-region entropy and finite
min-cut diagnostics can agree while reconstruction-visible and channel-visible
geometry differ.

The dS capstone is the observer-semantics packaging of this arc. It emphasizes
finite observer access and horizon semantics rather than boundary subregion
reconstruction.

## Difference From AdS/HaPPY Semantics

AdS/HaPPY-style toy codes usually privilege an asymptotic boundary and ask which
boundary interval reconstructs a bulk region. This capstone does not use that
primitive. Its primitive is a finite observer patch, its shared horizon, and the
static diamond generated by the observer union. The relevant diagnostics are
finite-region entropy, observer algebra, erasure complementarity, and channel
fixed points.

That is why the result is dS-like: it abstracts observer horizon semantics. It
is not literal de Sitter geometry.

## What This Teaches Quantum Gravity

The technical lesson is that entanglement- or horizon-visible data are not
enough to determine observer-accessible geometry. In finite QEC models, two
realizations can agree on all named horizon and patch entropy diagnostics while
differing in the logical algebra available to observers and in the channel
semantics of erasure and fixed points. A quantum-gravity-inspired reconstruction
claim should therefore be checked against operator algebra and channel
semantics, not inferred from entropy data alone.

## Limitations

This is a finite stabilizer/QEC toy model, mostly `k=1`. It does not construct
continuum de Sitter space, classify all causal-patch grammars, prove a universal
no-cloning theorem, or give an asymptotic dS/QG model. The exhaustive statements
are exhaustive only for their declared finite families and bounded rule
languages. The certified statement is narrower and more rigorous: a finite QEC
toy model satisfying a de Sitter-like observer-patch diagnostic gate exists.
