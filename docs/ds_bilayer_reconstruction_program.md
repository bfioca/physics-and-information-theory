# Finite Static-Patch Bilayer Reconstruction Program

## Scientific target

The near-term target is not a full dS/CFT correspondence. It is a finite,
falsifiable theorem about two de Sitter-like observer screens:

> For a specified family of two-screen quantum states or codes, determine when
> a bulk operator algebra is reconstructible from the north screen, the south
> screen, both screens jointly, or neither screen, and relate transitions in
> that reconstruction net to independently derived generalized-entropy data.

An ER=EPR interpretation is earned only after both sides are present:

1. an operational statement about recoverable operator algebras or channels;
2. a geometric or gravitational statement derived from the same state family.

Entanglement, a graph min-cut, or a connectivity permutation by itself is not a
wormhole theorem.

## Finite problem specification

Let

```text
V_lambda : H_code -> H_N tensor H_S tensor H_aux
```

be a parameterized encoding isometry. `N` and `S` are finite north/south screen
systems. `H_aux` contains degrees of freedom inaccessible to the declared
observer. For every screen region `R`, define:

- `S_lambda(R)`: the entropy of the normalized code state on `R`;
- `L_lambda(R)`: the logical Pauli/operator subspace supported on `R`;
- `epsilon_lambda(R, A)`: the best recovery error for logical algebra `A`;
- `G_lambda(R, X)`: a generalized-entropy score for assigning bulk algebra
  `X` to `R`, when a geometric prescription has actually been supplied.

The complete finite reconstruction object is the net

```text
R -> L_lambda(R), epsilon_lambda(R, A).
```

Comparisons quotient physical screen relabelings and changes of logical Pauli
basis. A claimed entropy/reconstruction separation must survive those
equivalences. This removes label artifacts that affected earlier benchmarks.

## Implemented certificates

### 1. Explicit teleportation channel

`qgtoy.quantum_channel` constructs Bell-projection and feed-forward Kraus
operators directly. It verifies trace preservation, Choi rank, Pauli transfer,
entanglement fidelity, and the preserved operator algebra.

For two mouths:

- aligned resource plus identity decoder is the identity channel;
- crossed resource plus pairing-aware decoder is the identity channel;
- crossed resource plus identity decoder is the correlated
  `II/XX/YY/ZZ` twirl.

The last channel has no perfect fixed-port qubit and no joint noiseless qubit,
but it preserves a two-generator classical center. Therefore the number of
fixed points is a fixed-port transmission count, not quantum capacity.

For a three-cycle with the wrong decoder, the explicit channel preserves one
collective noiseless qubit generated by global `XXX` and `ZZZ`. This is a useful
warning: global channel structure cannot be inferred by summing single-port
diagnostics.

### 2. Label-invariant entropy/reconstruction search

`qgtoy.bilayer` compares:

- the complete entropy function on every physical region, modulo all physical
  qubit permutations; and
- the complete net of region-supported logical Pauli subspaces, modulo the same
  physical permutations and all six one-qubit logical Pauli basis changes.

The exact bounded scan finds no discordance in:

| family | `n` | codes checked | entropy classes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| all permutation-inequivalent `k=1` stabilizer codes | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| same | 2 | 9 | 2 |
| same | 3 | 75 | 5 |
| same | 4 | 750 | 13 |
| deleted-check codes from local-Clifford graph-state representatives | 5 | 33 | 26 |

This is a bounded negative result. It does not prove that complete entropy data
determine reconstruction, but it makes that rigidity question a concrete first
theorem/no-go target:

> Does the complete entropy function of a finite `[[n,1]]` stabilizer code
> determine its supported-logical-subspace net up to physical permutations and
> logical Clifford equivalence? If not, what is the smallest counterexample?

Either outcome is publishable at the appropriate scale if proved cleanly. A
counterexample becomes relevant to holography only if its equivalence quotient,
operational recovery distinction, and geometric interpretation are nontrivial.

### 3. Tunable two-screen transition calibration

The first transition model is one coherent flagged isometry that routes an
input qubit to the north screen with probability `p` and to the south screen
with probability `1-p`. Tracing either output leg gives the corresponding
screen channel. Choi comparison verifies that these state-derived channels are
exactly the standard complementary quantum erasure channels. Explicit recovery
maps show:

- perfect south reconstruction at `p=0`;
- equal average recovery fidelity `3/4` at `p=1/2`;
- perfect north reconstruction at `p=1`.

This is an operational QEC calibration, not yet de Sitter physics. The next
scientific step is to derive `p`, or an analogous competition between recovery
saddles, from a static-patch state and a generalized-entropy prescription.

### 4. Exact recovery/quantum-area matching theorem

For a maximally mixed input qubit, the reduced screen entropies of the coherent
routing isometry are

```text
S(N) = h_2(p) + p,
S(S) = h_2(p) + 1 - p.
```

The replacement-decoder entanglement fidelities are

```text
F_e(N) = p + (1-p)/4,
F_e(S) = 1-p + p/4.
```

Therefore

```text
S(N)-S(S)       = 2p-1,
F_e(N)-F_e(S)   = 3(2p-1)/4.
```

In the symmetric finite analogue with equal bare screen areas, the recovery
winner and the screen with larger `A+S(screen)` exchange at exactly `p=1/2`.
The two calculations are independent outputs of the same isometry: one uses
reduced-state entropy, the other uses an explicit recovery channel.

The exact erasure-channel capacities sharpen the statement:

```text
Q(N) = max(0, 2p-1),
Q(S) = max(0, 1-2p).
```

This is the finite item-5 theorem. It mirrors the bilayer de Sitter proposal's
criterion that the screen with larger quantum area encodes the exterior and
that encoding transfers when the quantum areas become equal. It is not a
derivation of that prescription from gravity.

There is also a useful no-go. If the north-minus-south bare-area bias is
`delta`, the quantum-area crossing moves to

```text
p_QA = (1-delta)/2,
```

while the recovery crossing remains `p_rec=1/2`. Thus a nonzero independent
area bias breaks the match. A nontrivial de Sitter model must make its channel
dynamics respond to the geometric area data; adding an area term after the fact
does not work.

### 5. ER=EPR and dS/CFT interpretation audit

At `p=1/2`, the finite model has:

- one bit of north/south entanglement for a pure payload;
- two bits of north/south mutual information for the maximally mixed payload;
- exact joint reconstruction from the inverse isometry;
- complementary individual-screen channels whose capacities exchange at the
  symmetric point; and
- an exact match between recovery and the symmetric quantum-area analogue.

That is enough to call the construction a **finite algebraic ER=EPR analogue**.
It is not enough to call it ER=EPR in de Sitter. The current model has no QES
derived from a gravitational action or constraint system, no controlled
semiclassical limit, no causal or traversable inter-screen protocol, and no
boundary CFT dictionary or scaling limit. Therefore it does not yet advance
dS/CFT.

The next required model must derive both the screen quantum-area difference and
the routing/recovery channel from one controlled static-patch construction. The
publishable target is to prove that the finite matching identity survives with
quantified corrections, rather than to relabel the symmetric erasure channel as
geometry.

## Route toward an ER=EPR-in-dS result

1. Choose one controlled dS setting: static-patch quantum fields with a finite
   regulator, a tensor-network model derived from a stated path integral, or a
   solvable lower-dimensional dilaton-gravity analogue.
2. Define the observer algebras and state family before defining geometry.
3. Compute recovery errors or relative-entropy preservation for north, south,
   and joint screens.
4. Independently compute candidate QES/generalized-entropy saddles.
5. Prove that the reconstruction transition and saddle exchange agree, with an
   error bound that survives the regulator or a controlled scaling limit.
6. Only then ask whether the joint state admits an ER=EPR interpretation and
   how it relates to a proposed dS/CFT boundary description.

The present branch completes finite analogues of steps 5 and 6. Step 5 is exact
only for the symmetric state-derived entropy competition and produces a no-go
for an independent area bias. Step 6 concludes that the finite algebraic
analogue passes, while the geometric de Sitter and dS/CFT gates fail.

The strongest realistic paper would provide a new theorem linking a
two-observer reconstruction transition to a de Sitter generalized-entropy
transition in a controlled model. The finite code is then the exact laboratory
and regression suite, not the headline claim.

## Novelty and kill criteria

Proceed only if at least one of these becomes true:

- a new label-invariant entropy/reconstruction theorem or counterexample;
- a state-derived two-screen recovery transition with quantitative error bounds;
- a derived match between recovery and generalized-entropy saddle exchange;
- a genuinely new obstruction to static-patch holographic reconstruction.

Stop or reframe if the result remains a renamed erasure channel, a connectivity
permutation, a standard stabilizer-distance fact, or an entropy collision that
disappears after relabeling.
