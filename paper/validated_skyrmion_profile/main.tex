\begin{abstract}
We give a computer-assisted proof for a massive
$SU(2)$ Skyrme hedgehog on a fixed de Sitter static background with a prescribed
spherical Dirichlet boundary.  Unlike prior rigorous work on the flat,
whole-space Skyrmion, the validated problem is massive, positively curved, and
confined at finite radius.  At dimensionless parameters
$\mu^2=1$, $\lambda=1/400$, and $x_{\mathrm w}=4$, an exact-rational
Newton--Kantorovich certificate proves existence and local uniqueness in an
augmented ball of radius $1/250$.  The certified solution is strictly
decreasing, its wall slope lies in
$[-0.09465,-0.08746]$, and its dimensionless rotor inertia lies in
$[21.149,48.921]$.  Invertibility of the certified linearization also gives a
locally unique real-analytic branch on an open neighborhood of the mass,
curvature, and wall-radius parameters, although no numerical width is claimed.
A second authenticated certificate proves a fixed-wall
radial frequency gap $\widehat\omega_{\rm rad}\geq1/5$.  Finally, exact
endpoint jets and interval bounds on six third-derivative norms give the sharp
inertia-transform asymptotic $\mathcal N^{(k)}(p)=O(p^{-3})$ for
$k=0,1,2$, with nonzero oscillatory leading coefficient fixed by the boundary
second jet.  This tail is boundary-induced and is not presented as an intrinsic
ultraviolet prediction of an unsupported Skyrmion.  We claim neither global
uniqueness nor a self-gravitating, nonspherically stable, dynamically supported,
or smoothly confined solution.
\end{abstract}
