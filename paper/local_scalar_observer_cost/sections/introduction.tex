\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Gapless two-level detectors linearly coupled to free fields provide an exact,
nonperturbative dephasing model.  Their time-ordered evolution is a Weyl
displacement because free-field commutators are c-numbers.  Landulfo used this
structure for relativistic communication channels~\cite{Landulfo2016};
Barcellos and Landulfo derived the associated field-energy and switching-work
accounting~\cite{BarcellosLandulfo2021}.  The same solvable detector now appears
in nonperturbative treatments of Danielson--Satishchandran--Wald (DSW)
decoherence~\cite{BatistaEtAl2026}.  Killing-horizon decoherence, its local
two-point-function description, and its de Sitter specialization are also well
developed~\cite{DSW2023,GrallaWei2024,DSW2025,Li2025}.

Those results determine the dephasing functional for a chosen source.  We ask
a different variational question: after the interaction has ended, how large
can the exact thermal dephasing exponent be at fixed field energy and fixed
final Cauchy support?  Compact support is essential because the thermal state
has arbitrarily soft modes.  Localization replaces the missing spectral gap
by a compact compression of the thermal inverse Hamiltonian.

We first solve this problem without imposing a relation between temperature
and geometry.  Let $h_0=\sqrt{-\partial_x^2}$ on the Dirichlet half-line, let
momentum data $p$ be supported in $[0,L]$, and set
\begin{equation}
 \Gamma_{\beta,p}=\ip{p}{h_0^{-1}\coth(\beta h_0/2)p},
 \qquad E_p=\frac12\norm{p}^2.
\end{equation}
Our first result is the exact optimum
\begin{equation}
 \Gamma_{\beta,p}\leq E_p\mathcal C_\beta(L),
 \qquad
 \mathcal C_\beta(L)=2L\Lambda(\pi L/\beta),
 \label{eq:general-intro}
\end{equation}
where $\Lambda(\tau)$ is the top eigenvalue of the explicit positive compact
kernel
\begin{equation}
 k_\tau(u,v)=\frac1\pi
 \log\frac{\sinh[\tau(u+v)]}{\sinh[\tau\abs{u-v}]}
 \quad\hbox{on }L^2(0,1).
 \label{eq:kernel-intro}
\end{equation}
The top eigenvalue is simple and its eigenfunction is strictly positive.  The
answer has the global bracket
\begin{equation}
 \max\left\{\frac{3L}{\pi},\frac{16L^2}{\beta\pi^2}\right\}
 \leq\mathcal C_\beta(L)
 \leq\frac{4\operatorname{arsinh}(1)}\pi L
      +\frac{16L^2}{\beta\pi^2},
 \label{eq:general-bracket-intro}
\end{equation}
with uniform remainders that identify the vacuum-controlled small-support law
and the thermal large-support law.

We then specialize to the conformally coupled massless scalar in a
four-dimensional de Sitter static patch.  The Bunch--Davies inverse temperature
is $\beta=2\pi R$.  Optical partial waves have
\begin{equation}
 A_\ell=-\partial_x^2+\frac{\ell(\ell+1)}
 {R^2\sinh^2(x/R)}.
\end{equation}
Resolvent order shows that no higher angular momentum has a larger thermal
momentum form.  A separate localized estimate controls the field-coordinate
sector, and the two lower bounds in \cref{eq:general-bracket-intro} dominate
that estimate for every $L/R>0$.  Consequently the half-line momentum
optimizer is the unique optimum over the full angular and canonical phase
space.  For $y=L/R$,
\begin{equation}
 C_{\rm opt}(y)=2y\Lambda(y/2),
 \qquad
 \log\frac1{2\eobs}\leq\EK R C_{\rm opt}(y),
 \label{eq:main-intro}
\end{equation}
where $\eobs$ is the halved diamond distance from complete pointer dephasing
and $\EK$ is the post-switch scalar Killing energy generated by the prescribed
source.  Temperature first changes the dimensionless small-support cost at
cubic order, and $8y^2/\pi^3$ is the sharp leading large-support coefficient.

Sharpness in \cref{eq:main-intro} is a statement about final Cauchy data
supported in $[0,L]$.  If a source is confined to areal radius $a<R$ for a
static-time interval of length $T$, finite propagation gives only
\begin{equation}
 L=R\operatorname{arctanh}(a/R)+T
 \label{eq:source-envelope-intro}
\end{equation}
as a causal envelope.  The resulting source-radius and duration inequality is
valid, but we do not claim that every fixed smaller source cylinder approaches
the top eigenfunction.  Smooth compact sources do approach the optimum when
their worldtube approaches the final-support ball.

We do not claim a new detector channel or a new positive-kernel variational
principle.  The candidate contribution is the exact finite-support thermal
covariance operator, its reduction from the full de Sitter angular and
canonical phase space, and its sharp support asymptotics.  Largest-eigenvalue
and isoperimetric questions for Riesz-potential operators have an established
literature~\cite{RuzhanskySuragan2016}; whether the particular conjunction
above is already implicit in that literature is the decisive novelty question.

The term field-energy requirement is literal.  The quantity $\EK$ does not
include the work, clock, trap, battery, or material stress needed to implement
the prescribed source.  The shared smeared qubit is an idealized detector, not
an autonomous relativistic probe field.  Complete pointer dephasing is also
only an operational channel target; the error in \cref{eq:main-intro} is not a
gravitational encoding error and is not identified with observer entropy.

Two comparisons are kept secondary.  First, the dephasing form is
incomparable with the local coherent-state entropy entering recent
Klein--Gordon Bekenstein bounds~\cite{HollandsLongoMorsella2026}.  Second, an
appendix records exact flux-free Einstein--scalar final-slice constraint data
for the same spherical scalar datum.  That appendix does not derive the
channel on a perturbed geometry or solve a coupled evolution.
