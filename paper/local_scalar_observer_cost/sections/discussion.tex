\section{Discussion and claim boundary}
\label{sec:discussion}

We have solved the sharp coupling-free localization--energy optimization for
an exact local pointer channel in the de Sitter static patch.  Three features are worth
separating.

First, the channel is nonperturbative but not new.  Its value here is that the
same compact source determines the pointer error, the coherent field stress,
the Killing-energy excess, and the causal support.  This prevents a resource
ledger assembled from unrelated effective models.

Second, compact support is the decisive hypothesis.  The static-patch KMS
state has arbitrarily soft modes.  Without a support or duration restriction,
no energy gap controls the dephasing exponent.  Localization replaces that
missing gap by a compact thermal inverse operator.  The exact formula
\cref{eq:sharp-cost} and remainder bounds
\cref{eq:small-support-exact,eq:large-support-exact} show that both the linear
small-support law and quadratic large-support law are intrinsic to the model.

Third, the gravity result is a final-slice statement.  For the flux-free
spherical subclass, the conformal field map identifies the optical energy
measure with the exact Einstein--scalar constraint mass measure.  This is
stronger than assigning an unrelated spherical mass profile, but weaker than
deriving the complete detector protocol in semiclassical gravity.

\subsection{What is and is not established}

The results establish:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
 \item the exact reduced pointer channel and same-source post-switch field
 energy and renormalized-stress excess;
 \item the exact all-angular optimum \cref{eq:sharp-cost}, its unique s-wave
 momentum sector, and a valid source-radius and duration envelope;
 \item sharp small- and large-support asymptotics and smooth compact source
 sequences approaching the optimal Rayleigh quotient;
 \item exact flux-free spherical Einstein--scalar Hamiltonian and momentum
 constraint data and a nonempty local weak-constraint example; and
 \item an explicit proof that the dephasing exponent and localized
 Klein--Gordon Bekenstein entropy are incomparable quadratic forms.
\end{enumerate}

They do not establish:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
 \item the stress, clock energy, or size of an autonomous actuator that
 realizes the prescribed source;
 \item an autonomous relativistic pointer field whose local degrees of freedom
 reproduce the single smeared qubit operator $Z_P$;
 \item the pointer channel on the perturbed geometry, a lapse solution, or a
 coupled Einstein--scalar evolution through the switching interval;
 \item a universal energy cost of measurement, observer complementarity, a
 de Sitter holographic dictionary, or ER=EPR;
 \item a new exact detector channel, a new DSW decoherence mechanism, or the
 first localization--information--energy bound in QFT;
 \item a closed elementary formula for $\Lambda(\tau)$ at finite support; or
 \item exact controllability of the sharp final-support optimizer from every
 strictly smaller source cylinder with the same causal output radius.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Next mathematical and physical questions}

The sharp constant is the simple top eigenvalue in
\cref{eq:dimensionless-kernel}.  The coordinate comparison proves that no
second optimization is missing.  \Cref{fig:cost-spectrum} supplies a
converged numerical realization and optimal-profile plot.  A future
interval-certified discretization could turn those estimates into tighter
rigorous finite-$y$ brackets, but it would not change the theorem.

The most important physical extension is perturbative stability of the
channel under the final metric distortion.  A controlled result would compare
the Bunch--Davies two-point function and propagator on the pure de Sitter
background with those on the small-$Q$ Einstein--scalar development, while
including a conserved model for the switching apparatus.  Until then, the
constraint result should remain a corollary rather than the title claim.

A complementary extension is to replace the shared smeared qubit by a
localized relativistic probe field, following the detector-from-field
framework of \cite{PercheEtAl2024}, and then ask whether the exact
localization--energy optimization survives the additional probe modes and
their stress tensor.

A second extension is geometric.  The proof uses a centered ball and the
positivity of the de Sitter angular potential.  The operator formulation
suggests an analogue on other static spacetimes in terms of localized norms of
$h^{-1}$ and $h^{-2}$.  Such a generalization would need to preserve the exact
channel/stress relation and to state carefully which energy is generated by
the local coupling.

Finally, the comparison in \cref{prop:bekenstein-separation} suggests that
local modular entropy and environment-induced dephasing probe complementary
aspects of localization.  Establishing useful inequalities after adding a
boundary-distance constraint, a restricted source family, or a finite
resolution could connect the operational detector problem more directly to
modular-energy methods.
