\section{Discussion and claim boundary}
\label{sec:discussion}

The main result is an exact final-support optimization, not a new detector
channel.  At arbitrary inverse temperature, the half-line momentum problem is
governed by the single compact operator $\mathcal K_{\pi L/\beta}$.  In the
conformal de Sitter model, angular resolvent order and a separate
field-coordinate estimate show that this momentum problem is the full
canonical and angular optimum.  The temperature-curvature relation is used in
that second step, not in the general half-line theorem.

Compact final support is the decisive hypothesis.  Without it, arbitrarily
soft thermal modes prevent an energy norm from controlling the dephasing
exponent.  With it, the compressed inverse Hamiltonian is bounded and the
exact coefficient has both a vacuum-controlled small-support regime and a
thermal large-support regime.  The uniform remainder estimates in
\cref{thm:thermal-half-line} make those statements quantitative.

\subsection{What is sharp}

The results establish:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
 \item the exact arbitrary-$\beta$ half-line momentum coefficient
 $\mathcal C_\beta(L)=2L\Lambda(\pi L/\beta)$, its unique optimizer, global
 bracket, and support asymptotics;
 \item the conformal de Sitter reduction of every angular and canonical sector
 to the unique s-wave momentum optimizer;
 \item the exact pointer channel and the post-switch scalar-field energy and
 renormalized-stress excess generated by the same prescribed source;
 \item a causal source-radius and duration envelope for the final support; and
 \item smooth compact sources approaching every final-support Rayleigh
 quotient when the source worldtube approaches the final-support ball.
\end{enumerate}

Sharpness refers to the optimization over final Cauchy data supported in
$[0,L]$.  For a source cylinder with spatial radius $\ell<L$ and duration
$T=L-\ell$, finite propagation proves the same upper bound but not
near-controllability of its top eigenfunction.  The title and main theorem do
not assert that stronger statement.

The field-energy quantity is equally specific.  It is the post-switch scalar
Killing energy of the coherent displacement.  It does not include the work,
clock, battery, trap, or material stress needed to realize the externally
prescribed source.  The smeared qubit is a standard gapless-detector
idealization rather than an autonomous relativistic probe field.  Accordingly,
the theorem is not a universal measurement-energy principle.

\subsection{Novelty boundary}

Exact gapless-detector channels, their thermal damping factors, and their
field-energy accounting are prior art.  Positive compact kernels, simple top
eigenvalues, and Riesz-potential variational principles are also established
mathematics.  The candidate contribution is their specific conjunction here:
the reflected thermal kernel selected by final support, the exact
general-temperature coefficient, the full de Sitter sector reduction, and the
uniform support asymptotics.  Independent review in both relativistic
detector theory and integral-operator or fractional-Sobolev theory remains the
binding publication gate.

The localized Klein--Gordon entropy comparison in \cref{sec:comparison} is
supporting evidence that the dephasing quadratic form is not a repackaging of
a recent Bekenstein bound.  It is not a second headline.  Likewise,
\cref{sec:gravity} is an appendix application to final-slice constraint data.
It does not derive the pointer channel on a perturbed geometry, include
actuator stress, or solve a coupled Einstein--matter evolution.

\subsection{Extensions}

The most direct mathematical extension is the true fixed-source-cylinder
optimization.  It requires either a near-controllability theorem for compact
sources in a prescribed smaller worldtube or a new source-to-final-data
operator whose singular value replaces $\Lambda$.  A broader geometric
extension would identify conditions under which localized norms of
$h^{-1}\coth(\beta h/2)$ admit comparable sharp constants on other static
spacetimes.

The cleanest physical extension is to replace the shared smeared qubit by a
localized relativistic probe field, following the detector-from-field
framework of \cite{PercheEtAl2024}.  Such a model would have to retain the
localization theorem while accounting for additional probe modes and their
stress tensor.  Neither extension is required for the narrow theorem proved
here.
