# External Specialist Shortlist

This is an author-facing routing list, not a claim that anyone is available or
has agreed to review. Contact one person per domain at a time and use public
institutional or publication contact channels.

## Detector/QFT Review

### First route: Andre G. S. Landulfo

Why: Landulfo derived the exact nonperturbative channel used by the manuscript
and coauthored its field-energy and switching-work accounting
([channel](https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.06641),
[energy cost](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.13896)). He is best placed to say
whether optimizing exact dephasing at fixed post-switch energy and final
support is actually new in that channel class.

Ask specifically: does the new variational problem follow from the earlier
energy formulas, and is post-switch field energy a physically useful resource
notion in this restricted setting?

### Second route: T. Rick Perche

Why: Perche works on localized detector models and their validity in curved
spacetime. His [current Stockholm University profile](https://www.su.se/english/profiles/ripe4726)
provides a public contact route, and the manuscript already compares with his
localized-QFT detector work.

Ask specifically: is the prescribed smeared qubit boundary adequate for the
narrow theorem, and would a localized probe-field realization be mandatory
for publication?

### Third route: a Batista--Landulfo--Mann--Matsas author

Why: their [nonperturbative DSW detector paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00956)
is the closest current bridge between exact detector dynamics and horizon
decoherence.

Ask specifically: is the fixed-support optimization distinct from their
finite-time exact treatment, and does the de Sitter specialization add useful
physics rather than repackaging the KMS factor?

## Operator-Theory Review

### First route: A. A. Polosin

Why: Polosin's [2022 finite-interval logarithmic-convolution paper](https://doi.org/10.1134/S0012266122090099)
contains the exact vacuum parent operator and analyzes its even and odd
sectors. The [journal page](https://sciencejournals.ru/view-article/?a=DeqRus_2209009Polosin&j=deqrus&n=9&v=58&y=2022)
links an open full text; the publisher record supplies a public corresponding-
author route.

Ask specifically: does established finite-interval convolution theory make
the `-log sinh` thermal odd sector, its principal norm, or the two uniform
remainders immediate?

### Second route: Sheela Verma or Jiya Rose Johnson

Why: Johnson and Verma study the same inverse pseudo-hyperbolic-distance
kernel on two-dimensional domains. Verma's
[IIT (BHU) profile](https://www.iitbhu.ac.in/dept/mat/people/sheelamat)
provides a public contact route; their [2026 preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20431)
establishes the precise technical overlap.

Ask specifically: does their largest-eigenvalue theory extend directly to the
weighted geodesic measure `dr/(2 tau r)`, or is that a distinct spectral
problem?

### Third route: Michael Ruzhansky or Durvudkhan Suragan

Why: they work on principal eigenvalues and spectral geometry of Riesz-type
integral operators. Ruzhansky's [official page](https://ruzhansky.org/)
provides current public contact channels.

Ask specifically: is the thermal support theorem a routine positive-kernel
application once the odd-convolution and hyperbolic representations are
known, and are the explicit uniform remainders substantial?
