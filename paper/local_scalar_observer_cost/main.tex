\begin{abstract}
The exact Weyl-displacement channel of a prescribed gapless detector is known.
We solve a finite-support optimization left open after that channel is fixed.
For Dirichlet half-line momentum data supported in $[0,L]$, with thermal
inverse temperature $\beta$, dephasing exponent $\Gamma$, and field energy
$E=\norm{p}^2/2$, we prove
\[
 \Gamma\leq E\mathcal C_\beta(L),
 \qquad
 \mathcal C_\beta(L)=2L\Lambda(\pi L/\beta).
\]
Here $\Lambda(\tau)$ is the simple top eigenvalue of the positive compact
kernel
$\pi^{-1}\log[\sinh(\tau(u+v))/\sinh(\tau|u-v|)]$ on $(0,1)$.  We give
global two-sided bounds and uniform small- and large-support remainders.  For
the conformally coupled massless scalar in a four-dimensional de Sitter static
patch, $\beta=2\pi R$, and the same s-wave momentum profile is the unique
optimizer over all angular and canonical sectors.  Thus final Cauchy support
$L=yR$ implies
\[
 \log\frac1{2\eobs}\leq\EK R C_{\rm opt}(y),
 \qquad C_{\rm opt}(y)=2y\Lambda(y/2),
\]
where $\EK$ is only the post-switch scalar Killing energy.  A source radius
and duration provide a causal upper envelope for $L$; sharp controllability
from every smaller fixed source cylinder is not claimed.  Smooth compact
sources approach the final-support optimum.  We do not include the work or
stress of an autonomous actuator or claim a total measurement-energy cost.
\end{abstract}
